# 006 — Product Economics (P&L Model)

## 1. Purpose of the Layer

The **Product Economics (P&L) layer** defines how the product generates revenue, incurs costs, and produces financial outcomes.

It describes:

- revenue streams
- cost structures
- cost and revenue drivers
- unit economics
- economic constraints and guardrails
- economic impact of changes

It answers:

- How does the product make money?
- Where do costs come from?
- What drives revenue and expenses?
- What are the economic limits and constraints?
- What is the economic impact of product behavior and changes?

---

## 2. Layer Role in AISMM

This layer connects:

```text
Value → Money → Decisions
```

It translates value creation into economic results and constraints.

Other layers rely on it to:

- evaluate product viability
- optimize cost vs value
- prioritize investments
- assess profitability
- enforce economic constraints

This layer defines **product-level economic logic and constraints**.

## 4. Core Concepts

### 4.1 Revenue

Income generated by the product.

---

### 4.2 Cost

Resource consumption required to operate or develop the product.

---

### 4.3 Cost Driver

Element that causes cost.

---

### 4.4 Revenue Driver

Element that generates revenue.

---

### 4.5 Economic Unit

Defines measurement basis (per user, per action, etc.)

---

### 4.6 Economic Constraint

Defines limits on the system:

- budget limits
- cost ceilings
- pricing constraints
- resource limits

---

### 4.7 Unit Economics Guardrail

Defines acceptable economic thresholds:

- cost per unit
- revenue per unit
- margin targets

---

### 4.8 Economic Impact Assessment

Evaluates economic effect of:

- changes
- features
- initiatives

## 16. Technical Economics Bridge

This layer defines product-level economics: value targets, revenue model, product P&L expectations.

**Technical-layer economics** — infrastructure costs, per-service costs, token/inference costs, and carbon accounting — are modeled in Bundle 12 (FinOps and Technical Economics).

Cross-layer flow:

```text
b0.006 product margin target → constrains → b12.1205 service unit cost thresholds
b12.1204 cost_of_incident → informs → b0.006 product economics model
b12.1203 inference cost → feeds → b0.006 AI feature unit economics
```

See [`b12-finops-and-technical-economics/README.md`](../b12-finops-and-technical-economics/README.md).
